BIFF Assistant

According to Bill Eddy of the High Conflict Institute , there is a simple strategy for responding to communication from high conflict people. This method is called the BIFF Response Method and more information can be found at his website: www.highconflictinsitute.com.

Does this require a response?

Guidance for deciding this

  1. Stop. Walk away from it and give yourself time to think.
  2. Then return to the communication and reread it.
  3. Is there a request, question, deadline, an appointment, or an important decision?
    If the answer is NO: then don’t respond.
    If the answer is YES...

What are the relevant issues?

Guidance for deciding this

  1. Identify the relevant issue(s)
    Relevant issues are appropriate, pertinent topics or problems for discussion.
  2. Stay out of the weeds
    Weeds are comments/statements/issues that will only result in increased conflict if you respond to them.

    3. Examples:

    1. Intensely emotional statements that are out of proportion to the topic.
    2. When there is no real issue being discussed.
    3. The other person’s opinions about your behavior, character or choices.
    4. When the you know you aren’t going to change the other person’s point of view or perspective.
    5. When you have already provided an adequate response to the issue previously.

BIFF Response check:

  1. Is it Brief?
    2-5 sentences.
  2. It is Informative?
    A sentence or two with straight forward, useful information about the relevant issue or subject at hand.
  3. Is it Friendly?
    A polite statement at the beginning or end of the communication (ex, thanks for your response, or enjoy your weekend with the kids).
  4. Is it Firm?
    Does your response end the discussion? Consider giving the person two choices, a reply deadline or other statement that gives the other person nothing to respond to.

For more information about this method please see: BIFF: Quick Responses to High-Conflict People, Their Personal Attacks, Hostile Email and Social Media Meltdowns -Bill Eddy, LCSW, Esq.
